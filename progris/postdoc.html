Postdoc position:

Projecting discharge from the Greenland ice sheet using climatic forcings derived from atmosphere-ocean models

 

Job description

Of all ice bodies on Earth, the Greenland ice sheet will likely contribute most to global sea level rise over the next decades and centuries. "Projecting discharge from the Greenland ice sheet using climatic forcings derived from atmosphere-ocean models" (ProGrIS, PI: Professor Ralf Greve) is a research project funded by the Japan Society for the Promotion of Science (JSPS) with the objective to assess this contribution, based on atmosphere-ocean and ice sheet modelling. ProGrIS is closely linked to the international ISMIP6 initiative and the Japanese ArCS project.

The main task of the position is to carry out numerical simulations of the ice dynamics and mass balance of the Greenland ice sheet with the ice sheet model SICOPOLIS, forced by climate scenarios for the recent past and the future. Spin-ups in order to provide suitable initial conditions for these simulations must also be conducted. Desirable model improvements include schemes for basal hydrology, ice stream dynamics, implementation of new DEMs (digital elevation models) for the ice surface and bed, and program optimisation (numerical techniques, computing speed). Emphasis can be placed depending on individual interest. Cooperation with the domestic and international project partners is required, and dissemination of results in the form of peer-reviewed publications and presentations at scientific conferences is expected.

Qualifications

Candidates should hold a PhD/doctoral degree in environmental/atmospheric/cryospheric sciences, oceanography, physics, geophysics or related natural sciences. Experience in numerical modelling with finite differences, good computer programming skills (preferably in Fortran) and a reasonable command of oral and written English are a prerequisite.

About the institute

The position is based at Hokkaido University's Institute of Low Temperature Science (ILTS) in Sapporo, Japan. ILTS was founded in 1941 as the first research institute affiliated with Hokkaido University. Since the latest reorganisation in 2008, ILTS is composed of three research divisions (Frontier Ice and Snow Science, Water and Material Cycles, Environmental Biology), a Joint Research Division and the Pan-Okhotsk Research Center. ILTS is approved by MEXT as a national Joint Use/Research Center and promotes comprehensive research of various natural phenomena occurring in the cryosphere and cold environments.

The successful candidate will join the Glacier and Ice Sheet Research Group of ILTS's Frontier Ice and Snow Science Division.
